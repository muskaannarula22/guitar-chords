RHYTHM VS. LEAD

There are two main styles of guitar playing: lead and rhythm. Lead guitar involves the guitar playing that "drives" the music. It's the melody that makes the song distinctly memorable and is only playing in parts of the song where there is no singing. Rhythm guitar refers to the supporting chords and provides a backdrop to the singer. I will be focusing mostly on rhythm playing. While most people want to practice lead guitar, it's important not to forget to learn rhythm. You can always play a song without the lead, but never without the rhythm.

TAB NOTATION

The base of any rhythm playing are chords. Once you have mastered pressing down strings, and plucking melodies on the bottom three strings, you're ready to learn a couple chords.

The best way to show the alignment of chords is with Tab Notes. While understanding sheet music enhances your understanding of music, most guitarists use tabs to learn chords. Its notation shows the shape of the chord which can be very helpful with memorization.
