WHY TAB NOTATION

To play string instruments like the guitar, ukulele or even bass, tab notation is usually enough. It is a rather straightforward approach to understanding the orientation of the left hand. You can literally mimic the shape shown to determine the orientation your hand needs to be. So, for all you visual learners out there, TAB IS YOUR BEST FRIEND.

The issue with sheet music is that it's not perfectly translatable onto the guitar (each of the guitar frets is a half-step while the distance between each note in the musical alphabet varies). When you look at the alphabet on a piano, they are all nicely organized because of the black keys (as shown in the picture of the piano below). But when it is put on the guitar (also shown below, it gets all fumbled up and confusing... unless you're willing to memorize that is.

Otherwise, Tab Notes are a wonderful start! And as you expand your musical knowledge, definitely look into adapting to sheet music. It will help you better understand the formation of music and scales, chord progressions and such.

READING TAB NOTATION

When showing the shape of a chord, this is the tab notation that is used. Each of the vertical lines represents a string from the guitar. The left most string is the top most string on the guitar. It is also the lowest string. The top-most guitar string is also called the 6th string and the bottom-most highest string is called the 1st string.

TABS WITH MELODIES

Often times, you will see sheet music accompanied with tab notes. Again, the concept is the same as above. The 1st (bottom most string on the guitar) is the top most line on the notes. And 6th string is the top most on the guitar but bottom most on the tab. The is because the lines on the tab notes is organized from highest to lowest, but on the guitar, it is lowest to highest when going top to bottom. It's wordy, I know. The numbers represent the fret you should have your finger on when plucking it. 0 means open string. Sometimes, you'll see three notes all in one row. This means you should play all three strings at once. I'd recommend always playing the top most one with your thumb and the rest with your other fingers.
